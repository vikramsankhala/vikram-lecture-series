Extend
the Source

Every signal starts somewhere. These extensions give your chain richer, higher-quality inputs — physical media, instruments, broadcast feeds, and analogue sources.

B1
← Source · Analogue
Vinyl Turntable via RCA
The K5 Pro ESS has stereo RCA inputs. A turntable with built-in phono preamp plugs directly into it — giving you an analogue vinyl source feeding a reference digital monitoring chain.
  1. 1
    Choose a turntable with a built-in phono preamp (look for "built-in phono stage" in specs). The AT-LP120XUSB has one and a USB output too.
  2. 2
    Run RCA (L/R) cables from the turntable's line-level output (not phono) to the K5 Pro ESS rear RCA inputs.
  3. 3
    Press INPUT on K5 Pro ESS to cycle to the RCA input. Volume is now controlled by the K5 Pro knob.
  4. 4
    Optional: Run the turntable's USB output into your PC simultaneously — digitise vinyl records in real-time using Audacity while listening through the HD 490 PRO.
₹12,000 – ₹45,000 Audio-Technica AT-LP120XUSB · Pro-Ject Debut Carbon EVO
B2
← Source · Digital
CD Transport via Coaxial/Optical
CDs are 16-bit/44.1kHz — bit-perfect lossless. A CD player with coaxial or optical output feeds the K5 Pro ESS directly, bypassing any internal cheap DAC in the CD player entirely.
  1. 1
    Get a CD player with coaxial S/PDIF output (preferred over optical — less jitter). Even budget CD players have this.
  2. 2
    Run a 75Ω coaxial RCA cable from the CD player's "Digital Out (Coax)" to the K5 Pro ESS's coaxial input on the rear.
  3. 3
    Switch K5 Pro ESS to Coaxial input. The K5 Pro's ESS DAC now handles all digital-to-analogue conversion — massively upgrading the CD player's sound.
₹3,000 – ₹8,000 Sony CD-P players · Marantz CD5005 · Yamaha CD-S300
B3
← Source · Network
Network Streamer / Raspberry Pi
Replace your laptop as the computer source with a dedicated streaming device. A Raspberry Pi running Volumio or Moode Audio streams Tidal, Qobuz, Spotify, and local files, outputting USB audio directly to the K5 Pro ESS.
  1. 1
    Flash Volumio OS onto a microSD card using Raspberry Pi Imager. Insert into a Raspberry Pi 4.
  2. 2
    Connect Raspberry Pi's USB port → K5 Pro ESS USB-B input. Volumio auto-detects it as the audio output device.
  3. 3
    Configure Volumio via browser (access at volumio.local). Add Tidal Connect, Qobuz, or point to your NAS-stored FLAC library. Now your chain runs 24/7 without a laptop open.
  4. 4
    Enable bit-perfect output in Volumio: Settings → Playback Options → "Volume Normalisation" OFF → "Enable Audio Resampling" OFF. Pure signal to the K5 Pro.
₹4,500 – ₹8,000 Raspberry Pi 4 (2GB) + case + microSD + power supply
B4
← Source · Professional
Audio Interface + DAW as Source
Add a Focusrite Scarlett or SSL 2 before your K5 Pro chain. The interface handles microphone inputs, instrument recording, and routes all audio through your DAW — the K5 Pro ESS then serves as the monitoring amp.
  1. 1
    Connect interface to PC via USB. In your DAW, set the audio interface as Input and Output device.
  2. 2
    Route DAW output to the interface's line outputs. Connect a TRS cable from the interface's line out to K5 Pro ESS RCA input (use a TRS-to-RCA cable).
  3. 3
    Set K5 Pro to RCA input. Now your DAW's master bus feeds the K5 Pro → HD 490 PRO directly. This separates your recording interface (low latency, mic preamps) from your monitoring amp (high quality, K5 Pro).
₹8,500 – ₹20,000 Focusrite Scarlett Solo · Scarlett 2i2 · SSL 2+
B5
← Source · Wireless
Bluetooth Receiver → K5 Pro (RCA)
Add a high-quality Bluetooth receiver between any wireless device and your K5 Pro ESS. Stream wirelessly from multiple devices — tablet in bed, phone across the room — while the K5 Pro handles the DA conversion and amplification.
  1. 1
    Get a BT receiver with aptX HD or LDAC output — FiiO BTA30 Pro (₹8,500) is ideal: it has coaxial output that feeds the K5 Pro ESS coax input, maintaining a digital path.
  2. 2
    Connect BTA30 Pro coaxial out → K5 Pro coaxial in. Pair your phone to the BTA30 via LDAC. The signal goes: Phone (LDAC Bluetooth) → BTA30 (digital decode) → K5 Pro (DA conversion) → HD 490 PRO.
  3. 3
    For pure analogue BT receiver: Use RCA out from receiver → K5 Pro RCA in. Switch K5 Pro to RCA input. Less ideal (double DA conversion) but functional for casual use.
₹3,500 – ₹9,000 FiiO BTA30 Pro · EarStudio ES100 Mk2 · Qudelix-5K
B6
← Source · Retro
Cassette Deck / Reel-to-Reel via RCA
Any analogue tape source with RCA line outputs plugs directly into the K5 Pro ESS's RCA inputs. Hear your cassette or reel-to-reel tapes through a reference chain — the HD 490 PRO will reveal every tape hiss artefact and warm analogue saturation in stunning detail.
  1. 1
    Connect tape deck LINE OUT (not headphone out) via RCA cable to K5 Pro ESS RCA inputs. Headphone outputs have the wrong impedance and level.
  2. 2
    Digitise simultaneously: Run a Y-splitter from the tape deck's RCA output — one path to K5 Pro (for monitoring), one path to a USB audio interface → PC (for recording). Archive your tapes in 24-bit/96kHz FLAC.
₹0 (if you own one) – ₹5,000 Vintage cassette decks from local electronics stores

Extend
the Output

The signal doesn't have to stop at your ears. These forward extensions branch your chain into rooms, recording devices, second listeners, and the physical world.

F1
Forward → Room
Nearfield Monitors via RCA Line Out
The K5 Pro ESS has a fixed RCA line output on the rear. Connect powered studio monitors here — now you have simultaneous headphone AND speaker monitoring from one chain. Switch by unplugging headphones or using a speaker switch box.
  1. 1
    Run RCA cables from K5 Pro ESS rear LINE OUT to your monitors' RCA or TRS inputs. Most monitors need a TRS-to-RCA cable.
  2. 2
    Set monitor volume on the speakers themselves — K5 Pro ESS's volume knob only controls the headphone output, not the line out level. Keep monitors at a fixed level and use the monitor's own volume for room listening.
  3. 3
    Add a speaker/headphone switch box (Nobsound Little Bear MC100, ₹3,500) between the K5 Pro line out and your monitors — lets you instantly toggle between headphone and speaker monitoring without replugging anything.
₹25,000 – ₹55,000 Adam T5V pair · Yamaha HS5 pair · KRK Rokit 5 G4 pair
F2
Forward → Amplification
Dedicated Headphone Amp (Separates)
Use the K5 Pro ESS purely as a DAC — run its line out into a separate, dedicated headphone amplifier. Separating the DAC and amp stages is a major upgrade path. The HD 490 PRO will reward a high-quality amp with better dynamics and blacker backgrounds.
  1. 1
    Set K5 Pro ESS to "fixed line out" mode — check the firmware / switch on rear. This bypasses the volume control, outputting a clean line-level signal to the amp.
  2. 2
    Connect RCA → dedicated amp input. Plug HD 490 PRO into the amp's 6.35mm output. Volume is now controlled by the dedicated amp.
  3. 3
    Choose a balanced amp for maximum benefit — Schiit Magnius, Topping A90 Discrete, or Burson Soloist 3X GT. Balanced output via 4-pin XLR gives double the power and significantly lower crosstalk than single-ended.
₹22,000 – ₹1,80,000 Schiit Magnius · Topping A90D · Burson Soloist 3X GT
F3
Forward → Archive
Line Out → Recording / Archiving
Tap the K5 Pro ESS's RCA line output to simultaneously record anything you're listening to — vinyl digitisation, tape archiving, live radio capture, or creating reference recordings of your mixes.
  1. 1
    Run a Y-splitter or RCA to dual RCA from K5 Pro line out — one path to your monitors/headphones (via amp), one path to an audio interface's line input for recording.
  2. 2
    In Audacity or your DAW, set the recording input to your audio interface's line in. Hit record. You now have a real-time capture of the analogue output — perfect for vinyl digitisation at 24-bit/96kHz.
₹0 (software) + ₹8,500 Focusrite Scarlett Solo for the interface input
F4
Forward → Second Listener
Headphone Splitter / Second Listener
Add a headphone amplifier/splitter after the K5 Pro ESS to drive two headphones simultaneously. Useful for collaborative listening sessions, teaching, or comparing two different headphones on the same source.
  1. 1
    Connect a headphone distribution amp (Behringer HA400, ₹3,000) to the K5 Pro ESS line out via RCA → 3.5mm cable. The HA400 has 4 headphone outputs, each with independent volume.
  2. 2
    Plug HD 490 PRO into output 1 (via UGREEN adapter). A second headphone into output 2. Both listeners hear the same source independently controlled.
  3. 3
    Note: The HA400 adds its own amplifier stage — quality is decent but not reference-grade. For critical dual-monitoring, use the K5 Pro headphone out for the HD 490 PRO, and a separate amp for the second pair.
₹3,000 – ₹12,000 Behringer HA400 · Mackie HM-4 · ART Headamp4
F5
Forward → DSP
Hardware DSP / Room Correction Processor
Insert a hardware DSP unit between the K5 Pro line out and your monitors. Units like the miniDSP DDRC-22D apply Dirac Live room correction in real time — turning a mediocre listening room into an acoustically corrected space.
  1. 1
    Connect K5 Pro line out → miniDSP DDRC-22D input via RCA. Connect miniDSP output → powered monitors.
  2. 2
    Run the Dirac Live measurement: place a calibrated microphone (included) at your listening position. Dirac maps your room's acoustic fingerprint and generates a correction filter.
  3. 3
    Apply the filter. Your monitors now sound as if they're in a treated room — corrected for reflections, standing waves, and room modes. Toggle on/off to hear the difference.
₹25,000 – ₹55,000 miniDSP DDRC-22D with Dirac Live licence
F6
Forward → Analysis
Measurement Rig — Know Your Sound
Build a headphone measurement rig using the K5 Pro ESS as the signal generator. With a GRAS or DIY coupler and REW software, you can measure the exact frequency response of your HD 490 PRO — validating your EQ and identifying channel imbalances.
  1. 1
    Download REW (Room EQ Wizard) — free. Set K5 Pro ESS as output. Use K5 Pro ESS's own line input (via a loopback) or a USB microphone as the measurement input.
  2. 2
    Run a sweep: REW generates a sine sweep through the K5 Pro → headphones → microphone captures the output. The resulting graph shows your headphone's actual response on your head in your room.
  3. 3
    Compare against the Sennheiser reference curve available from their website. Apply compensating EQ in EqualizerAPO. You are now your own acoustic engineer.
₹0 (REW is free) + ₹2,000–₹8,000 Calibrated mic (MiniDSP UMIK-1 · Dayton EMM-6)

Innovations & Improvisations

Unexpected
Things to Try

These aren't standard use cases. They're creative improvisations — ways to use this chain that its designers probably didn't envision, but that work brilliantly.

I — 01
Binaural Recording Playback Rig
KA13 · 4.4mm balanced · head-tracked HRTF · Waves NX
+
What it does

Binaural recordings — made with two microphones placed in a dummy head's ears — create a 3D sound experience that's specifically designed for headphone playback. The HD 490 PRO's wide soundstage and the K5 Pro's transparent DAC make this experience extraordinarily immersive. Combined with Waves NX's head-tracking (using your webcam), the soundstage stays fixed in space as you move your head — exactly like real life.

Where to find binaural recordings: Qobuz has a binaural category. YouTube has hundreds of ASMR and binaural content. BBC Radio 3 has binaural broadcasts. Search "binaural" + any music genre.

Innovation: Route binaural content through Waves NX (₹5,000 standalone) with head-tracking enabled. The effect is so realistic that most people remove the headphones to check if someone is in the room.
Setup Steps
  1. 1
    Install Waves NX and enable webcam head-tracking. The software tracks your head position via camera and adjusts the HRTF in real time.
  2. 2
    Insert Waves NX as the last plugin in your DAW chain or use its standalone mode with the K5 Pro as output.
  3. 3
    Play binaural content and slowly turn your head. The sound source stays "in front of you" — the most convincing headphone 3D effect achievable without specialised hardware.
I — 02
Personalized HRTF Calibration
Sonarworks SoundID · Personal profile · HD 490 PRO target curve
+
What it does

Sonarworks SoundID Reference is professional headphone calibration software that measures your specific pair of HD 490 PRO headphones with a calibration microphone and creates a correction profile unique to your unit — compensating for individual driver variation (every headphone pair sounds slightly different).

The result: a completely flat, reference-neutral sound profile — not a generic EQ curve but one derived from your actual headphones. This is what professional mastering studios use.

Sonarworks SoundID Reference workflow
1. Download SoundID Reference (free trial) 2. Select "Sennheiser HD 490 PRO" profile 3. OR measure your specific unit (requires mic) 4. Enable correction in System Audio / DAW 5. K5 Pro ESS receives corrected signal

I — 03
The "Second Output" Trick — Simultaneous Monitors + Headphones
K5 Pro RCA out → monitors · Headphone out → HD 490 PRO · Instant AB switching
+
What it does

The K5 Pro ESS outputs audio simultaneously from its headphone output AND its RCA line output. This means both the HD 490 PRO and your studio monitors are driven at the same time — from the same source, at the same quality level.

For mixing, this is invaluable: hear a section on monitors (room perspective), then immediately switch to headphones by simply unplugging the headphone cable — no software or switch boxes needed for basic use. More advanced: add a Nobsound MC100 speaker switch to properly mute monitors when headphones are in use.

Improvisation: Use this for "check listening" — mix primarily on monitors, but every 10 minutes plug in headphones to check details (reverb tails, stereo width, low-end clarity) that the room masks. This is the standard professional workflow.

I — 04
DSD512 Native Playback — Hearing the Absolute Maximum
SACD ISO rips · foobar2000 + ASIO · K5 Pro native DSD512 decoding
+
What it does

The K5 Pro ESS supports native DSD512 — this is 512× the base DSD rate (22.4MHz sampling). Almost no consumer equipment supports this. Setting up native DSD512 playback is the absolute maximum your chain can decode, and the HD 490 PRO's 5–36kHz range is capable of resolving the differences.

DSD files sound different to PCM in an almost indescribable way — a quality of "flow" and tonal naturalness that high-bit PCM doesn't quite achieve. Whether this is "better" is subjective, but hearing it through this chain is extraordinary.

Setup Chain
  1. 1
    Install foobar2000 + SACD plugin + ASIO output plugin on Windows. All free.
  2. 2
    Install FiiO's USB ASIO driver from FiiO's website. This enables native DSD (not DoP) output to the K5 Pro ESS at DSD512.
  3. 3
    In foobar2000: Preferences → Playback → Output → ASIO → FiiO ASIO driver. SACD plugin: set output mode to "DSD" (not PCM). Play a .dsf or .dff file. The K5 Pro ESS front display shows the DSD rate.
foobar2000 DSD512 path
SACD ISO / .dsf file → SACD Plugin (native DSD) → ASIO output (FiiO driver) → K5 Pro ESS (DSD512 native decode) → HD 490 PRO Plus → Your ears (22.4 MHz sampling rate)
I — 05
The KA13 as a Portable Measurement Device
REW · Sweep generation · Acoustic measurement on the go
+
Unexpected Use Case

The KA13's high-quality DAC outputs are precise enough to use as a measurement signal generator. When connected to a laptop with REW (Room EQ Wizard), it sends calibrated sine sweeps through any connected speakers or headphones — and a USB microphone captures the response.

This is a portable acoustic measurement rig you can take anywhere — measure speakers at a friend's studio, check a room's response before buying monitors, or verify a headphone's frequency response on site. The KA13's low THD+N (<0.00012%) means your measurement signal is essentially perfect.

Innovation: Combined with a UMIK-1 USB microphone (₹7,500), your phone + KA13 + UMIK-1 becomes a professional acoustic measurement kit that fits in a jacket pocket.

I — 06
Convolution Reverb — Listen in Famous Rooms
Impulse responses · Carnegie Hall · Abbey Road · Real acoustic spaces
+
What it does

Convolution reverb plugins use "impulse responses" — recordings of actual rooms (Abbey Road Studio 2, Carnegie Hall, Hagia Sophia) — to place any audio inside those spaces in real time. Running this through the HD 490 PRO's reference-grade transducers creates a remarkably convincing illusion of acoustic space.

Free impulse responses: OpenAIR (University of Edinburgh) has 100+ real-world spaces as free IRs. Sampled by professional engineers in actual locations.

Setup
  1. 1
    Download a free convolution reverb plugin: Convolution Reverb (free in Logic Pro), OrilRiver (free VST), or Reaper's built-in ReaVerb.
  2. 2
    Download an IR pack from OpenAIR or Fokke van Saane's IR library. Load a famous room (e.g., "Tyndall Bruce Monument" or "R1 Nuclear Reactor").
  3. 3
    Route your audio through the plugin (in your DAW or with a VST host like Cantabile). Output to K5 Pro ESS → HD 490 PRO. Hear your music in Carnegie Hall.

I — 07
EQ Matching — Make Any Source Sound Like Another
EqualizerAPO · Tone matching · Headphone profile swap
+
What it does

Using parametric EQ (EqualizerAPO + Peace), you can apply any frequency response profile to the HD 490 PRO — making it sound like virtually any other reference headphone: the Sennheiser HD 800S, Audeze LCD-4, Sony MDR-Z1R, or AKG K712. AutoEQ has profiles for 2,000+ headphones.

This lets you audition the "sound signature" of ₹2,00,000 headphones on your ₹31,000 HD 490 PRO — before committing to a purchase. The HD 490 PRO's neutral starting point makes it an ideal canvas for EQ targets.

How to do it
  1. 1
    Visit AutoEQ.app and search for any headphone you want to "simulate." Download the Peace EQ preset.
  2. 2
    Load in Peace EQ (EqualizerAPO's GUI). The preset applies the target headphone's frequency curve to your HD 490 PRO's output.
  3. 3
    Compare profiles by switching presets mid-song. You're essentially A/B testing headphones without owning them.
Creative use: Create your own "dream headphone" by combining elements — the bass of an LCD-2, the mids of an HD 800S, the treble extension of an Utopia. Parametric EQ makes this possible on your existing HD 490 PRO.
I — 08
AI-Powered Upscaling — Enhance Low-Quality Audio
AudioSR · AI upsampling · Spotify → Hi-Res equivalent
+
Emerging Technology

AI audio upsampling is a rapidly growing field. Tools like AudioSR (research demo, 2024), iZotope RX's "Music Rebalance," and Accusonus ERA use machine learning to reconstruct high-frequency information lost in MP3/AAC compression — then output a 24-bit/48kHz file that genuinely sounds better than the original 320kbps MP3.

Your chain — with its resolution capability — is one of the few that can actually resolve the difference between AI-upscaled and original files. On earbuds, the difference is imperceptible. On the HD 490 PRO through the K5 Pro ESS, it's meaningful.

Current state (2025): AI upscaling is still imperfect — it sometimes introduces artefacts. Best used on older MP3 rips you can't replace with streaming. Not a substitute for lossless sources.
